Blog #23




𝐋𝐢𝐟𝐞 𝐇𝐚𝐜𝐤𝐬 𝐝𝐮𝐫𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐋𝐨𝐜𝐤𝐝𝐨𝐰𝐧



Hello everyone!

Remember your life in 2019, hugging people, going for work or even a simple walk, all was normal. We tend to take things for granted unless we are not able to do them. In November 2019, a storm of COVID-19 had begun which changed our lives, it made people learn that ‘𝐧𝐚𝐭𝐮𝐫𝐞 𝐩𝐚𝐲𝐬 𝐛𝐚𝐜𝐤’. However us, being humans have not realized it even now. Although this is for our safety, the waste generated during the lockdown period is almost all non-biodegradable containing plastic. The hand wash we use and its refills, hand sanitizer, masks, fruits and vegetable sanitizing liquids, and moisturizers all come in plastic bottles. In a year, approximately 𝟗.𝟒 𝐦𝐢𝐥𝐥𝐢𝐨𝐧 𝐭𝐨𝐧 𝐨𝐟 𝐩𝐥𝐚𝐬𝐭𝐢𝐜 𝐰𝐚𝐬𝐭𝐞 is generated in India and the alarming situation is that 40% of this waste is unattended. So think about the situation during lockdown.

It is anticipated in the United Nations Conference on Trade and Development that about 75% of the total waste will be solely of plastic. All this waste will eventually go into landfills or water bodies, causing land pollution and harm to innocent marine life which will impact the food cycle and eventually the ecosystem which we all are a part of.

So 𝐓𝐄𝐀𝐌 𝐙𝐄𝐑𝐎 𝐖𝐀𝐒𝐓𝐄 is here with the series called ‘𝐋𝐢𝐟𝐞 𝐇𝐚𝐜𝐤𝐬 𝐝𝐮𝐫𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐋𝐨𝐜𝐤𝐝𝐨𝐰𝐧’. We are bringing this to do our part in reducing the plastic waste. Stay tuned for further posts in the series.
