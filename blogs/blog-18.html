Blog #18




Share your story – 8.0



Here’s presenting you the story of Bharvi Pande who is recycling and reusing the things at her scale. Ancient Indian Traditions and Customs teaches us to make the best use of the best! I have always been listening from my Grandmother and Mother that nothing is waste. Everything can be recycled and reused and here is a list I have tried and still trying to inculcate the same in myself.

1. My father since my childhood took me to Panjrapol (A Gaushala in Mumbai) to feed cows and pigeons on my birthday but along with this I realised growing plants is really necessary for the environment since we take a lot from environment but do not give the same and hence instead of bringing bouquets home, I started bringing flower saplings home on every birthday of my family members. Also started preparing rooting powder for plants using sundried tea (used tea).

2 . Have you ever thought how cartons are made? They are made by cutting trees and whatever products we buy are packaged in the same carton which we usually throw away thinking it is a waste but as taught by my grandmother and mother I started preparing trosseaus such as ring trousseau, or gifting articles from the same as shown in the pictures above.

3. We all have heard that plastic destroys the environment as it take hundreds and thousands of years to break it down. But is it possible to live without plastic? I feel no as still directly or indirectly we use plastic. I started planting plants in the plastic we bring home which brings positivity and greenery to my home.

4. We can definitely try to stop plastic. I tried to contribute my bit by using bamboo straw, bamboo toothbrush and instead to using plastic earbuds, started using traditional ear cleaner made of brass.

5. The lockdown all over the country encouraged me to make my own face cleanser using multani mitti and rose water; my own shampoo using aritha, shikakai and aavla; and kumkumadi oil using chandan, kesar, coconut oil and almond oil instead of using chemical based products. I am grateful to my parents who inculcated all these things in me, all my gurus who have helped me throughout, all my family members and friends who have always encouraged me.

- Story shared on 28 July
